Highlights of WCLS’s “Chinese Week” theatrical performances

September 17-23, 2023 is New Zealand’s annual Chinese Week. Wellington Chinese School was invited by the city library to organize a theatrical performance at the Newlands Community Center to celebrate Chinese Week with the local community and promote Chinese language and culture.

Lin Shuyue and Yang Xinyi from Book 12 of the school, Zhuge Ziqi from Book 10 and Jin Zhuoxin from Book 7 showed us the beauty of Chinese by reciting "Chinese Language"!

Chinese is the language with the largest number of speakers and the oldest history in the world, with a history of more than 6,000 years. Throughout this long history, Chinese and overseas Chinese have used Chinese to record their lives and express their emotions.

"The supreme king of martial arts slays the dragon with his precious sword. Who can compete with me if I rely on the sky to slay the dragon?" Everyone must still remember the opening theme song "The Sword Is Like a Dream" in the 93 version of "The Legend of Heaven and the Dragon Sword". Classmate Liang Jiadong will use his piano music to interpret the joyful grievances and grievances in the world described by Mr. Jin Yong.

Home is the harbor of our souls, the cradle of our growth, and our shelter from wind and rain. With mother's education and father's protection, we can thrive in a happy and beautiful environment. Corbin and Tiffany from Book1B class recited the poem "Song of Happiness"
