WCLS successfully held a lecture on Peking Opera culture

On a cold winter day, the Peking Opera culture lecture held by Wellington Chinese School was filled with enthusiasm. Teacher Shi Juan from the Chinese School and Teacher Ma Bingyang from Yafeng Club vividly explained the characteristics of Peking Opera and had enthusiastic interactions with the teachers and students on site.

Through several vivid and exciting Peking Opera clips, the use of oars instead of boats and steps to show waves in the fishing and killing family, the bare-handed threading of needles in Picking up Jade Bracelets, the use of flags instead of water in The Legend of White Snake and four people to show a thousand troops, and three Teacher Shi gave us a very intuitive analysis of the dark fight under bright lights in the fork, and gave us a taste of the unique charm of the virtual characteristics of Peking Opera.

Special guest teacher Ma Bingyang from Yafeng Society demonstrated the electronically simulated Peking Opera soundtrack on the spot, and warmly invited students from the Chinese school to come on stage to experience the simple use skills and rich expression content of the prop riding whip. Children of all ages actively participated in the interaction and had a great time practicing.

The atmosphere was once again enlivened through Teacher Shi’s easy-to-understand explanation of the stylized characteristics of Peking Opera. Teacher Ma also demonstrated the Peking Opera masks of Shengdan Jingchou, and demonstrated the electronic simulation effects of various literary and martial arts accompaniments one by one. The two lecturers continued to invite the students to come to the stage to interact. Whether it was the joy, anger, sorrow, and happiness of the old bearded students, or the delicate orchid fingers and the fluttering sleeves of the beautiful lady, everyone participated enthusiastically, had a happy experience, and left something to be desired.
